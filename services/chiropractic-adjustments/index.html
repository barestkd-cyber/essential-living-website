Chiropractic Adjustments

Every treatment includes an adjustment, done by hand or with a gentle instrument. Traditional hands-on adjusting is what Dr. Josh and most of our patients prefer.

His primary techniques are Diversified and Gonstead, and he is also trained in Thompson, Sacro-Occipital (SOT), Flexion/Distraction, Impulse, and Activator methods. That range matters, because there is no cookie-cutter way to adjust everyone.

Dr. Josh has adjusted newborns just hours old and patients in their 90s. Whatever your age or situation, the adjustment is matched to you.

Why people come see us

Most people find a chiropractor for a reason. A back that seized up at work, a neck that won't turn, headaches that keep coming back, an old injury that flares up every hunting season. Others come in feeling fine and want to stay that way. Whatever brings you in, the first visit starts with a real evaluation, not assumptions.

About the techniques

Diversified is the classic hands-on approach most people picture: specific, controlled adjustments delivered by hand. Gonstead adds a detailed analysis to find the exact segment that needs attention before anything gets adjusted. Thompson uses a drop-piece table that assists the adjustment with less force. Activator and Impulse are instrument-assisted methods that deliver a precise, low-force adjustment through a small handheld tool, often a good fit for older patients, young kids, or anyone who prefers a gentler touch. Flexion/Distraction uses a specialized moving table to gently stretch and decompress the spine.

Your doctor chooses the approach based on your body, your history, and your comfort, and they explain the why before they start.

Common questions

Does an adjustment hurt?

Most adjustments don't hurt, and many patients describe immediate relief. Some mild soreness afterward can happen, similar to how you feel after a good workout. Tell your doctor what you're feeling and they'll adapt.

What is the popping sound?

That sound is gas releasing from the fluid inside the joint as pressure changes, the same thing that happens when you crack your knuckles. It is not bone grinding or anything breaking, and an adjustment can be effective with or without it.

Do I have to keep coming back forever?

No. Your doctor gives clear recommendations and the decision is always yours. Some patients come in for a specific problem and are done when it resolves. Others choose ongoing wellness visits because of how they feel. Both are welcome here.

Do you adjust kids and seniors differently?

Yes. Pressure, positioning, and technique all scale to the person. Dr. Josh has adjusted newborns just hours old and patients in their 90s, and no two adjustments look the same.
